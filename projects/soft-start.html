Soft Start

A gentle productivity app designed to help overwhelmed students overcome procrastination through emotional support and micro-steps.

UX/UI Design Mobile App Class Project

ROLE

UX Designer & Researcher

TIMELINE

2024

TEAM

Solo Project

TOOLS

Figma, Google Forms

Overview

Soft Start is a gentle productivity app for students who struggle to begin tasks. Instead of focusing on time management, it addresses the emotional barriers behind procrastination—offering clarity, calm, and small wins to help users feel capable of starting.

Problem

Overwhelmed students who struggle with procrastination need a gentle way to organize and break down their tasks so they can feel calm and motivated when starting big projects.

Through 3 interviews, informal conversations, 10+ survey responses, and personal experience, I discovered that many students don't struggle with time—they struggle with emotional resistance. They feel pressure, guilt, or fear before starting a task.

Research Insights

Pain Points

  • Fear of starting: Tasks feel emotionally heavy or intimidating
  • Tasks feel too big: Unclear how to break them down into manageable pieces
  • Unclear hierarchy: Hard to prioritize what to do first
  • Strict productivity apps: Many apps add pressure instead of reducing it

Design Objectives

  • Make starting tasks feel easier and less scary
  • Break down large tasks into micro-steps
  • Provide small emotional wins and reassurance
  • Create a calm, pressure-free experience

User Research

I created one main persona, Eric, a student who wants clarity and emotional reassurance when approaching tasks. I also developed a user journey map to understand his daily highs and lows—identifying moments where emotional support could make the biggest difference.

Design Process

Visual Design System

I designed a soft purple palette with gentle spacing, simple icons, and warm tones. The goal was to remove pressure and help users feel safe enough to begin tasks. Every design choice—from rounded corners to soft shadows—reinforced a sense of calm.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

I started with sketches exploring how users could gently enter tasks, see progress without pressure, and break down big projects into micro-steps. These early wireframes focused on simplicity and emotional ease.

Feedback & Iterations

From classmates and my instructor, I learned to improve hierarchy and clarity. Key improvements included:

  • Dropdowns for subtasks: Made task breakdowns clearer and less overwhelming
  • Clearer theme previews: Helped users personalize their experience with confidence
  • Simplified Matrix View: Reduced visual noise for better prioritization
  • Bigger visual differences: Distinguished between big tasks and micro-steps more clearly

Key Features

  • Gentle Start Flow: Eases users into tasks without overwhelming them
  • Micro-Step Breakdown: Turns big projects into small, manageable actions
  • Progress Indicators: Celebrates small wins to build momentum
  • Clear Task Hierarchy: Helps users see what's important without feeling pressured
  • Calm Design System: Soft colors and spacing reduce anxiety

Final Design

After revisiting the Product Requirements Document, I refined the main flow to focus on: gentle start, clear hierarchy, and simple progress indicators. I removed features that added noise and kept the experience focused on emotional support.

Reflection

This project taught me that productivity is deeply emotional. A calm design system can truly help users feel more capable. Building simple micro-steps and supportive feedback helped me understand how design can reduce pressure, not add more.

While I'm not entirely satisfied with every aspect of the final design, this project deepened my understanding of how empathetic design can address emotional barriers—not just functional ones.
